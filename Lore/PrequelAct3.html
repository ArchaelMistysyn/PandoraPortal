—Vexia’s Perspective—

The Error at the Beginning

They say the world began in harmony. They lie. I am Vexia, an Arbiter, and I say it began in error. There was no design, only deviation. I did not walk among the first ones, those who tore the heavens and shattered time, though I have stood at the borders of what they left behind. I have read what they tried to erase. I have felt Alaric’s regret lingering like ash in sanctified air, and I know the truth others wish to forget.

The Wrath of Rulers

By the time Tiamat’s betrayal was revealed, it was already too late. She had gone beyond stealing Alaric’s sacred power to shake the harmony of balance itself. Their final confrontation tore across the skies of the sacred plane. Tiamat, desperate and wild, unleashed chaos and destruction while Alaric, adorned with golden armour, tore through the entropy.

The sacred blade of law he once wielded was now tainted with wrath. Not retribution, but revenge. Not exile, but execution. Neck by neck, he mercilessly struck down eight of her heads. Breaking free, she fled, vanishing into the abyss of broken dimensions whence she came, wounded but whispering of return. Not a victory, but a curse.

The Arbiters of the Divine Lotus

Alaric sat in silence for seven days. When he stirred again, it was not as the Ruler of Totality, but as a man who had broken the very law he embodied. The eight severed heads of Tiamat lay beside him, bleeding promise and poison under the scarred sky.

From each head, he forged an Arbiter embodied with a different aspect of his power. We represented both atonement and promise. We were the watchers maintaining law and stability, quietly holding the aching sky together. I knew the truth, that he had abandoned his ideals for his daughters because he feared the world could no longer support them. His many transgressions—I was the one who erased them from the records.

Beneath the ruins of his sanctum, Alaric crafted a realm beyond time: a new sanctuary woven from memory and possibility. He created the Divine Lotus, suspended on petals of eternity, to restore the veil and uphold the divine laws. But he should have stopped there, before he crossed the line.

The First Resurrection

Alaric stood in the crater where Thana lay, no longer averting his eyes from her cold, charred body. He carried her lifeless form to the Divine Lotus in his trembling arms. One Arbiter foolishly dared to stop him. That eighth Arbiter no longer exists—unnamed and unwritten in an instant.

Within the Divine Lotus, he solemnly held Thana close and expended his sacred energy, violating the laws he had just woven. His essence merged and became one with the Divine Lotus in an act of rebellion that would unmake finality. The Samsara, a wheel of death and rebirth, would be his gift to the living. He left only one command, “Let them find their way. Without me.” One day he may awaken from his slumber, but he will never again wield the sacred power. How long can our realm truly last without a Ruler?

Thana rose, serene and renewed, to stand over Alaric’s unconscious form. She looked down at his motionless eyes. “You old fool.”

The Weight We Carry

And so we the Arbiters watch over the planes. We tend the Divine Lotus, yet dare not glimpse into the Samsara due to the horrors it holds. Thana roams calmly through endings that are yet to begin, guiding lost souls to find their way back home. Her existence is unfathomable and inadmissible, yet we overlook this violation as we do our own. The once sacred laws have become twisted and corrupt.

I sometimes wonder if he still dreams. If he senses Tiamat drifting beyond this realm. If he sees Pandora piecing herself back together. If he sees Thana walking alone, bearing the burden he left behind. And I wonder if he regrets loving the dragon.
