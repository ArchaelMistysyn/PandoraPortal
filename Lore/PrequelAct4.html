—Eleuia’s Perspective—

When Harmony Cracked

We were not meant to fight, but I remember the day the stars turned against each other. Neither thunder nor fire heralded war, but whispers. Promises spoken softly through the Divine Lotus. Promises of peace. Promises of healing. Promises that fractured us.

We the Paragons, born of Pandora’s shattering, were many and different. We were not made to obey. We were made to be. We coexisted as fragments in fragile unity. The Arbiters, sculpted from Alaric’s grief and Tiamat’s remains, masked duplicitous intentions buried under flowery words. Their judgment cast long shadows across our freedom. We have never walked alongside each other as they governed structure while we sought freedom.

We called it oppression. And the divide became a chasm. The first war.

Pandora’s Box

Pandora, our beginning, longed to be whole again. Still broken, still lost. And the Arbiters circled the scent of blood like wolves. Beneath the Divine Lotus, they fed her lies and false promises laced with feigned sympathy.

'You are incomplete,' they told her. 'We can restore what you once were.' And she believed them. They gave her that dreadful box, a vessel capable of sealing even the divine. She returned to us, her Paragons, and asked us to lay down our shields. Some pleaded. Some rebelled. But me? I cried.

She raised her Hammer. No longer the unsteady child who had wildly struck Tiamat, she now wielded it with intent and control. One by one, she sealed us in that box. She couldn’t understand us, but we understood her. Despite our individual feelings, we knew she was not to blame. Our echoes followed her, trying to convince her, but in doing so we only added to the voices that tormented her.

A Sister’s Bond

Oblivia and Akasha were two Paragons endowed like myself with boundless abilities, yet paling in comparison to that Hammer of higher existence. Pandora sealed Akasha, while Oblivia vanished, escaped into the abyssal plane. That domain and the Paragon Rua, both born of Pandora’s own darkness, were now the very things that kept her at bay. She could not breach that bottomless chasm on her own. I truly believed she might finally give up this misguided hunt.

But Thana was watching too, and she saw what her sister had become and would come to be. The great light she had once admired now flickered dimly. She approached her sister as a stranger, an unknown wanderer.

‘Do you know who I am?’

Pandora didn’t answer. Did not utter the name that had once brought her comfort for she could no longer remember. Despite not knowing, there was an unspoken, instinctual trust. Thana took the box from her shaking hands and led a golden legion of undead from the Samsara into the abyss, returning with the sealed Paragons.

Before leaving, she asked Pandora, ‘Pitiful soul, is this really what you want?' I knew no answer would be forthcoming. I was her desire, her wish. It was my fault, for my very existence stole from her those feelings she so desperately needed. I watched from afar as Thana returned the box, but she did not return to the Samsara right away. Instead, she came to me.

‘Pandora, It was you I was asking. Think about it carefully. Don’t make the same mistakes that I once did, dear sister.’ It felt strange to be called by that name, but I think Thana was the only one who saw us for what we really were. Then, without hesitation, she reached out and hugged me. That was the first time I remember crying.

A Defiled Wish

Frozen with despair for so long, I forgot that I was the one who could change things, the only one who could show Pandora what she’d lost. I should’ve gone to her, become her hope. Instead I sought to end things at the source, so I took the fight to the Arbiters. I thought I could solve everything with my wish-granting ability, but I understood neither their power, nor my limits. I was powerless against them. I ran to her as she arrived—the first me—reaching for the hand that once was my own.

'It’s not your fault,' I said. 'Come back.' But it was too late. She pushed me away and drew her Hammer. They watched us with their voracious eyes. The hammer struck first, then recognition. Wide-eyed, I wished her Hammer someplace else. But they never feared me. They feared that weapon. They poisoned us against each other, using me to take it away from her. Before I could wish it back, they were upon us. That was the last thing I remember before we were both sealed.

Yubelle, the Adjudicator, Arbiter of Judgement, led the Arbiters into the Samsara to confront Thana. Outnumbered by the golden legion, Yubelle made her way through the hellish battlefield and stood alone against Thana. The two clashed amidst a war of blood and bones. Thana already knew the outcome, not only of this war, but also of the one to follow. A future that even Fleur, the Oracle, Arbiter of Prophecy, could not foresee. Overcome by divine lightning, Thana was defeated and sealed alongside her fragmented sister. With the war ended, the box was hidden in the mortal plane and lost to time. Echoes of Thana’s last words lingered in the air alongside the scent of death.

‘This is not the end, it is the beginning of yours, usurper.’

End of Act IV
